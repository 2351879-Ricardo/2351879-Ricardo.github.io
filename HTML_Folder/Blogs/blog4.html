What Interactivity Means On The WWW

"Towards a Study of Information Geographies" is a reading that I found highlighted well the consideration of access to information online and the general control of information in the world. It also further highlights how information is manipulated and controlled via power relations and access to said information.

(Im)mutable Augmentations

The reading points out that information on the internet can be considered as (im)mutable, highlighting the fact that it is both mutable and immutable. This can be clearly seen in our daily lives with how information is presented as well as who has access to said information - as well as who is in charge of distributing the information. If wanted, people could keep information immutable by having no further effect on the information (in terms of things such as editing) to provide a concise and unchanged form of media. However, this clearly leaves room for the information to be mutable.

In this day and age, the ability to edit, change and adapt information is becoming more apparent. A simple and rather simplistic example can be seen in Twitter's goal of making tweets able to be edited. Not only is information tending to be able to be more mutable in the physical sense, but is also reliant on change due to how people interpret said information as well as how information is presented to the reader. This point will further be discussed in the accessibility and power dynamic of information in later sections.

Controlling the Information

The reading points out that even before the information era, news and the spread of information were more prominent in the Northern territories and nearly inexistent in the Southern territories. This is directly related to the influence of power on an international level, as well as who is in charge of providing information. Information provided is also varied and hence reliant on the geological location. This can be seen in basically any country, as seen mainly with propaganda in wars. The government and people of power will only relay information that is beneficial to them, varying information from geological locations dependent on circumstances in the area.

Access to Information

While people like to point out there has been a betterment in the spreading of information as well as the access to it, it is still a prominent problem where there is unequal access. This stems from big corporations in all sectors. For a relative comparison, I will be using an example of the gaming industry. While people are able to access game servers from across the globe, people in foreign areas are less advantaged and have less impact on the system. This can be seen from examples such as the lack of game servers in South Africa. Another example is that if the server's conditions were to change in the host area (for example an American game server), regardless of other gaming communities from varying locations, the control of the server and how it changes is purely reliant on America. If they were to shut down the servers, it would be in regard to them and not taking into consideration other foreign communities.

This point further highlights the power imbalance still apparent in society. Just like times of old, the North still dominates with access and control to information. This is directly related to the already existing technologies as well as the vast differences in advancements in technologies between areas. As such, naturally, the North will still dominate.

A Move Towards Proper Mutability

Even still now, information is controlled by big corporations. Information is hard to change when provided by large corporations and it is hard to alter information and have fair equal access. In my personal opinion, the advancements towards open-source technologies is a step in the right direction, as information is fully accessible and changeable by the full community, able to be customized to specific parties.
